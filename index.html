FAQ

Common questions.

Where does the data come from? +
PropIntel aggregates from 7 verified sources: Regrid (county assessor parcel records), FEMA NFHL (flood zone), US Census ACS (demographics), People Data Labs (skip trace), ATTOM Data (AVM + sold comps + mortgage history for Pro), Geocodio (address normalization), and the FEMA Map Service Center as verification fallback. All sources are government databases or institutional-grade licensed aggregators.
What is the motivation score and how is it calculated? +
The motivation score (0–100) is built from five verified ownership signals: Absentee ownership (+25 pts), Out-of-state mailing (+20 pts), LLC/entity ownership (+10 pts), Long hold duration — 10+ years (+10 pts), and Tax delinquency (+15 pts). Scores 60+ are HIGH and indicate multiple overlapping signals — your best outreach targets. Full methodology →
What's the difference between assessed value and market value? +
Assessed value is the county's number for calculating property taxes — not what the property is worth. PropIntel always labels it "Tax Assessed Value." Market value is what a buyer and seller would agree to today in an arm's-length transaction. We show a market estimate range alongside the assessed value. In fast-appreciating markets like Dallas, assessed value can be 15–30% below actual market. Read more →
How do I read the FEMA flood zone? +
Zone X = minimal risk, no mandatory flood insurance. Zone AE = 100-year floodplain — flood insurance required for federally-backed loans, typically $800–$3,000+/year. Zone VE = coastal high-velocity, highest risk and cost. For Zone AE properties, always ask for an elevation certificate — it can dramatically reduce insurance premiums if the building sits above base flood elevation. All zone codes explained →
What does the deal analyzer actually calculate? +
Three acquisition scenarios — at assessed value, market low, and market high. For each: down payment (25% LTV), loan amount, monthly P&I, DSCR (net operating income ÷ annual debt service — lenders require 1.25x minimum), and $/SF vs. market comps. Use assessed to anchor your offer, market low as your negotiation ceiling, market high to understand your competition. Full methodology →
Do you cover all US states? +
Base parcel data covers all 50 states. Sold comps and AVM are strongest in Texas and major national metros, with coverage expanding. FEMA flood zone and census data are fully national. If a Pro report returns limited comp data in a specific market, that is noted in the report — you're never charged for a section with no data.
Can I use this for client-facing work? +
Yes. The shareable report link is clean and professional — safe to forward to clients, lenders, or partners. For agents, use the Starter as a listing presentation supplement. Enterprise accounts get full white-label with your firm's branding — clients never see PropIntel. Contact us for volume pricing.
What's the refund policy? +
If a report fails to generate or returns no parcel data, we refund immediately — no questions asked. If data is live, delivered, and accurate to public records, all sales are final. Double-check your address format before submitting.
